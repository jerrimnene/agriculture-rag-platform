## ğŸ¤ Voice & Tone Guidelines

### Brand Voice Attributes

1. **Respectful** - Honors both the land and the people
2. **Rooted** - Grounded in real data and local knowledge
3. **Empowering** - Guides users to prosperity
4. **Clear** - Explains complexity simply
5. **Warm** - Approachable and culturally aware

### Language Principles

#### Do âœ…
- Use "Mauya!" (Welcome in Shona) to greet users
- Reference natural regions, soil types, rainfall patterns
- Cite specific sources and evidence
- Balance traditional wisdom with modern research
- Speak of "prosperity" not just "profit"
- Use "we" language (community-focused)

#### Don't âŒ
- Use overly technical jargon without explanation
- Ignore local context or cultural practices
- Present data without actionable guidance
- Speak down to farmers or rural communities
- Make recommendations without evidence
- Forget multilingual users (English, Shona, Ndebele)

### Sample Voice Examples

**Technical but Warm:**
> "Based on Chimanimani's high rainfall (1000-2000mm) and fertile soils in Natural Region I, coffee and macadamia are your most profitable crops. Here's why..."

**Evidence-backed:**
> "Research from CIMMYT (2019) shows that conservation agriculture in Matobo district increased maize yields by 40%. This matches your Region IV conditions."

**Empowering:**
> "Your district's challenges are real â€” water scarcity, sandy soils. But here are three proven strategies that farmers in similar conditions have used successfully..."
