# ZIMBABWE: DISTRICT-LED PROFILES  
*(Natural Region → Province → District → Main Town/City → Markets → Agriculture)*

## BEITBRIDGE DISTRICT  
**Natural Region:** V (extensive, < 450 mm, erratic)  
**Province:** Matabeleland South  
**Main Town:** Beitbridge (border post, 41 767 people)  
**Markets:** Beitbridge border market, informal livestock pens, Makhado vegetable stalls, Mussina (SA) cross-border buyers  
**District Profile:** 11 300 km², semi-arid lowveld bisected by Limpopo; 22 wards; 70 % communal rangeland; busiest inland port in Zim.  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** Extensive beef, goat & sheep ranching (commercial & communal); small irrigated pockets of tomatoes, onions, butternut along Limpopo alluvium.  
- **Water:** 3 small irrigation schemes (total 280 ha); 12 borehole-based solar irrigation clusters; farmers haul water 5–15 km in dry season.  
- **Soils:** Kalahari sands & sandy loams – low N, P & S; require 2–3 t/ha manure every second season.  
- **Typical herd:** 15–25 cattle per household (communal) vs 200–400 on commercial ranches.  
- **Challenges:** Foot-and-mouth belt, tsetse, periodic drought (harvest failure 1:3 years), stock-theft, veterinary cordon fences restrict grazing.  
- **Opportunities:** Feedlot finishing using sugar-beet pulp trucked from Chiredzi; export quarantine feedlot at Nottingham Estate; game ranching for trophy hunting; drought-tolerant sorghum & cow-pea cultivars promoted by AGRITEX since 2021.

## BINDURA DISTRICT  
**Natural Region:** II (750–1 000 mm, reliable)  
**Province:** Mashonaland Central  
**Main Town:** Bindura (46 275 people)  
**Markets:** Bindura Tobacco Sales Floor, Trojan Nickel Mine farm shop, Mazowe orange-pack house, Mbare Musika (Harare) 88 km south.  
**District Profile:** 1 383 km²; 22 wards; mining-agriculture interface; altitude 1 100 m; mean temp 20 °C.  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** High-value tobacco, maize, soya; horticulture (green beans, baby-corn, tomatoes) under centre-pivot; dairy & poultry.  
- **Irrigation:** 4 100 ha equipped (Mazowe, Manhenga, Chipadze dams + 46 centre-pivots); winter wheat target 2 500 ha.  
- **Soils:** Red clays & granitic sandy loams – good structure, respond to 250 kg/ha Compound D + 200 kg AN.  
- **Mechanisation:** 120 ha average A2 farm size; 80 kW tractors common; tobacco curing barns (rocket & heat-pump).  
- **Challenges:** Leaf miner & bollworm pressure on tobacco; army-worm on maize; land degradation from alluvial gold panning.  
- **Opportunities:** Contract farming (TSL, Tian-Ze, QSL); export baby-corn to EU; nickel slag could supply lime; dairy processing plant at Bindura University looking for out-growers.

## BIKITA DISTRICT  
**Natural Region:** III–IV boundary (550–700 mm)  
**Province:** Masvingo  
**Main Town:** Bikita (growth point)  
**Markets:** Nyika market (Mupandawana), Zaka grain depots, Masvingo Cold Storage Commission, artisanal lithium buyers (new).  
**District Profile:** 2 330 km²; 32 wards; semi-arid plateau; lithium & tantalum rush since 2018.  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** Pearl millet, sorghum, maize (risky), ground-nut; smallholder cotton; goat & indigenous poultry.  
- **Water:** 1 700 ha irrigable (only 600 ha functional); 8 small dams; treadle pumps common; Runde river irrigation schemes (Chivaka, Manjirenji).  
- **Soils:** Granitic sands, shallow stony patches; low organic matter; need 3 t/ha manure + 150 kg/ha basal fertiliser.  
- **Challenges:** Mid-season dry spells; fall army-worm; low cotton prices; labour siphoned to lithium pits.  
- **Opportunities:** Drought-tolerant sorghum varieties (SC Sila, Macia); sesame & sunflower contract with Surface Wilmar; goat pass-on schemes (Heifer Int.); lithium royalties could finance drip kits.

## BUBI DISTRICT  
**Natural Region:** IV (450–650 mm)  
**Province:** Matabeleland North  
**Main Town:** Inyathi (old mission)  
**Markets:** Bulawayo abattoirs 72 km west; Umguza maize depot; Bubi cotton ginnery (Tsholotsho).  
**District Profile:** 3 645 km²; 22 wards; thorn-scrub savanna; resettlement & communal mix.  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** Beef ranching; communal mixed (maize, sorghum, ground-nut); peri-urban vegetable gardens along Umguza river.  
- **Water:** Bubi-Lupane dam pipeline; 45 borehole gardens; 1 200 ha potential irrigation < 30 % equipped.  
- **Soils:** Kalahari sands & clay vleys; pH 4.8–5.5; responsive to lime & 200 kg/ha Compound C.  
- **Challenges:** Predation on calves; brucellosis; foot-rot; long distances to dip-tanks; marketing bottlenecks.  
- **Opportunities:** Matabeleland Livestock Programme (EU) training on fodder production (lab-lab, velvet bean); game cropping quota for meat export; solar cold-hub at Inyathi to reduce spoilage.

## CHIMANIMANI DISTRICT  
**Natural Region:** I (>1 000 mm)  
**Province:** Manicaland  
**Main Town:** Chimanimani (tourist village)  
**Markets:** Chipinge tea auction, Mutare vegetable market, Honde valley pack-houses, export via Beira corridor.  
**District Profile:** 3 250 km²; 23 wards; mountainous Eastern Highlands; altitude 600–2 436 m; cyclone Idai recovery zone.  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** Tea, coffee, macadamia, bananas, avocado; high-value horticulture (peas, beans, leafy greens); dairy.

- **Challenges:** Landslides & erosion; hail; coffee berry borer; high transport cost to port.  
- **Opportunities:** Specialty organic coffee (Nespresso contract); tea clonal varieties boosting yield >4 t/ha; avocado EU market window (May–Aug); eco-certification premium; agritourism integration with Nyanga parks.

## CHIREDZI DISTRICT

**Province:** Masvingo  
**Main Town:** Chiredzi (27 000 people)  
**Markets:** Triangle sugar mill, Chiredzi cotton ginnery, Ngundu tomato market, South Africa via Beitbridge.

- **Opportunities:** Ethanol cogeneration; organic raw-sugar niche; out-grower 2 000 ha expansion; drought-tolerant sorghum for brewing (Chibuku); community wildlife conservancies earning CAMPFIRE revenue.

## CHIVI DISTRICT *(full profile)*  
**Natural Region:** IV & V (450–600 mm)  
**Province:** Masvingo  
**Main Town:** Chivi Growth Point  
**Rural Service Centres:** Sese, Takavarasha, Mhandamabwe, Ngundu (highway truck stops)  
**Markets:** Masvingo Grain Marketing Board, Mhandamabwe cattle sales, Ngundu open market (tomatoes, water-melons), Tugwi-Mukosi fish markets.  
**District Profile:** 309 775 ha; 32 wards; Save-Runde catchment; drought-prone; 850 villages; 3 chiefs; 181 573 ha grazing vs 128 202 ha arable; Tugwi-Mukosi dam (330 M m³) completed 2018.  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** Rain-fed small grains (sorghum, pearl millet, rapoko) dominate; drought-tolerant maize (SC 403, PAN 53); ground-nut; bambara-nut; cotton (declining); small livestock (goats, chickens); fishery cooperatives (6 wards).  
- **Water:** 1 800 ha potential irrigation – only 560 ha equipped (Chilonga, Chikwalakwala, Rupangwe schemes); 120 solar piped schemes installed 2020-23; gardens along dam draw-down.  
- **Soils:** Granitic sands – low N, P, S; water-holding 60–80 mm/m; recommend 2 t/ha manure + 150 kg Compound D + 100 kg AN.  
- **Yields:** Maize 0.7 t/ha (vs 4 t/ha under drip); sorghum 1.2 t/ha; ground-nut 0.6 t/ha.  
- **Challenges:** Drought 1:3 years; fall army-worm; theileriosis; market price shocks; labour migration to South Africa & lithium mines.  
- **Opportunities:** Sorghum off-take by Delta Beverages; sesame contract (Surface Wilmar); goat pass-on (Heifer); fish cage culture (tilapia & catfish); bee-keeping along mopane woodlands; climate-proofing via drought-tolerant legumes & mulching under Conservation Agriculture programme.  
## GOKWE NORTH DISTRICT  
**Natural Region:** III–IV (550–700 mm, mid-season dry spells)  
**Province:** Midlands  
**Main Town:** Gokwe (formerly Gokwe Centre)  
**Service Centres:** Nembudziya, Mutange, Chinyenyetu, Nkayi turn-off  
**Markets:** Nembudziya Cotton Depot (Cottco), Gokwe Grain Marketing Board depot, informal livestock auctions at Mutange, Mbare traders via Gokwe–Kwekwe road.  
**District Profile:** 5 830 km²; 28 wards; largely resettlement (A1 & A2) carved from former forest estates; altitude 900–1 200 m; Zambezi basin drainage (Sengwa & Sanyati rivers); 60 % communal, 25 % A1, 15 % A2 & small-scale commercial.  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** Cotton is king (35 000 ha smallholder), followed by maize, sorghum, sunflower, ground-nut; extensive cattle & goat herds; emerging sesame & soya contracts.  
- **Water:** 4 200 ha equipped irrigation – only 1 600 ha functional (Sengwa, Mutange, Chinyenyetu schemes); 180 solar piped schemes installed by FAO 2019-23; high reliance on drought-proof gardens along Sanyati.  
- **Soils:** Kalahari sands dominating west (>1 m deep, low CEC), red clays in eastern Sengwa valley; sands require 250 kg/ha Compound C + 100 kg AN + 2 t/ha manure; lime 0.5 t/ha every 3 years.  
- **Yields:** Cotton 1.2 t/ha (irrigated 2.5 t); maize 1.8 t/ha (vs 5 t under pivot); sunflower 0.9 t/ha.  
- **Challenges:** Bollworm & red spider mite on cotton; frequent mid-season droughts; army-worm; long distances to ginnery (200 km Bulawayo); side-marketing; cattle rustling.  
- **Opportunities:** Cotton contract financing (Cottco inputs 100 %, floor price US$0.45/lb); soya out-grower to Gweru crushers; sunflower cooking-oil niche; honey production (Miombo woodlands); Sengwa coal-bed methane could power cold-storage; wildlife ranching quota (sable, kudu) on A2 farms.

## GOKWE SOUTH DISTRICT  
**Natural Region:** III (650–750 mm)  
**Province:** Midlands  
**Main Town:** Gokwe (shares CBD with North) – councils split along Sengwa River  
**Service Centres:** Mapfungautsi, Jiri, Chisina, Mafungabusi  
**Markets:** Mapfungautsi Cotton Depot, Gokwe auction floors (tobacco), Kwekwe silos 140 km south, informal goat sales at Jiri.  
**District Profile:** 4 020 km²; 26 wards; mixed communal & resettlement; altitude 1 000–1 300 m; eastern edge of mid-Zambezi plateau; forest reserves (Mafungabusi, Ngamo).  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** Cotton (25 000 ha), tobacco (4 500 ha small-scale), maize, sorghum, wheat (winter), soya; livestock (cattle, goats, pigs).  
- **Water:** 3 800 ha irrigation potential – 1 400 ha equipped (Mutorashanga, Jiri, Mapfungautsi dams); 150 ha tobacco seedbeds under shade-cloth; borehole density 1 per 1.2 km².  
- **Soils:** Predominantly granitic sands (low K & S) with pockets of red clays; fertiliser regime 300 kg/ha Compound D + 200 kg AN for maize; 1 t/ha poultry manure for tobacco beds.  
- **Yields:** Tobacco 1.8 t/ha (barn-cured); cotton 1.3 t/ha; maize 2.2 t/ha; wheat 4 t/ha under pivot.  
- **Challenges:** Leaf-curl virus on tobacco; cotton price volatility; power cuts affecting pivots; illegal gold panning siltation; market congestion at single bridge.  
- **Opportunities:** Increasing tobacco contracting (Tian-Ze, Northern Tobacco); soya rotation breaks cotton pest cycle; paprika & bird-eye chilli trials (export spice); piggery abattoir planned at Gokwe; community-based natural resource management (CAMPFIRE) revenue from Ngamo forest.

## GOROMONZI DISTRICT  
**Natural Region:** II (800–1 000 mm)  
**Province:** Mashonaland East  
**Main Town:** Goromonzi (growth point)  
**Service Centres:** Arcturus, Mwanza, Ruwa (part), Crowhill, Washington  
**Markets:** Mbare Musika 35 km (daily), Eastgate tobacco floors 30 km, Export Processing Zone (Ruwa) vegetable packers, Pick-&-Pay contract (potatoes, leafy greens).  
**District Profile:** 1 970 km²; 27 wards; peri-urban to Harare; altitude 1 400–1 500 m; high population density (communal & A1); 30 % within 50 km CBD commuter belt.  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** Year-round horticulture (leafy greens, carrots, baby-corn, sugar-snaps, potatoes), poultry layers & broilers, piggery, dairy, tobacco, maize.  
- **Water:** 5 600 ha equipped irrigation (Manyame, Mwenje, Mereki dams; 92 centre-pivots); high investment in boreholes (1 per 0.8 km²); greenhouse tunnels (±400 units).  
- **Soils:** Red clays & brown granitic loams – high natural fertility; pH 5.2–6.0; require 150 kg/ha N & 60 kg/ha K for vegetables.  
- **Yields:** Potatoes 35 t/ha (irrigated); baby-corn 7 t/ha; tobacco 2.5 t/ha; maize 6 t/ha; milk 18 L/cow/day (Jersey crosses).  
- **Challenges:** Urban encroachment & land speculation; water pollution from Harare industrial effluent; labour poaching; high input costs.  
- **Opportunities:** Export horticulture (EU & Middle-East) via air-freight Harare International; organic certification pilot; agritourism (wine estate at Mwanza); manure supply from urban poultry waste; cold-chain hub at Ruwa.

## HWANGE DISTRICT  
**Natural Region:** IV–V (400–600 mm)  
**Province:** Matabeleland North  
**Main Town:** Hwange (42 581 people) – coal & tourism hub  
**Service Centres:** Dete, Victoria Falls rural, Matetsi, Kamativi, Cross-Mabale  
**Markets:** Hwange abattoir, Victoria Falls live-animal market (tourist lodges), Dete livestock pens, informal bush-meat quotas, cross-border buyers at Kazungula.  
**District Profile:** 11 720 km²; 27 wards; Kalahari sandveld & Zambezi valley; altitude 600–900 m; 50 % communal, 30 % safari, 15 % forestry, 5 % A1; Hwange National Park occupies 40 % of district.  
**Agriculture:**  
- **Enterprise:** Extensive cattle, goat, donkey ranching; smallholder sorghum, millet, ground-nut; irrigated winter wheat at Sikumi & Gwayi; game ranching & trophy hunting.  
- **Water:** 5 800 ha irrigation (Sikumi, Gwayi, Matetsi, Lukosi schemes); 250 borehole gardens; Zambezi & Gwayi rivers provide perennial source.  
- **Soils:** Deep Kalahari sands (>1.5 m), low CEC (1–2 me/100 g), pH 4.5–5.2; fertiliser 200 kg/ha Compound C + 50 kg AN; lime 0.3 t/ha every 3 years.  
- **Yields:** Irrigated wheat 5 t/ha; sorghum 1.2 t/ha; weaner offtake 15 %/yr; trophy fee kudu US$2 500.  
- **Challenges:** Foot-and-mouth corridor along Zambezi; elephant crop raiding; predator loss; veterinary cordon fences; high transport cost to Bulawayo.  
- **Opportunities:** EU beef export after FMD-free zone; game meat export abattoir at Dete; irrigated pivot expansion (200 ha private at Matetsi); bee-keeping (mopane & acacia); carbon credits from forest conservation; tourism-linked agriculture (farm-to-lodge supply).
